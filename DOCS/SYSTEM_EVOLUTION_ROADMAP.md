# SYSTEM EVOLUTION ROADMAP
*(Non-Regret, Non-Drifting, LLM-Deliverable)*

This document defines the **only acceptable staged evolution** of the VirLife system.

The roadmap is designed so that:
- early stages are **irreversible locks**,
- later stages are **purely additive growth**,
- no stage requires rework of earlier stages,
- semantic drift is structurally impossible,
- and the final system necessarily matches BASELINE.

This is **not** an implementation plan.  
This is a **constitutional roadmap**.

---

## DEFINITIONS

### LOCK STAGE
A stage that introduces **irreversible constraints**.
Once completed, it must never be modified or “enhanced”.

### GROWTH STAGE
A stage that adds new producers of meaning or pressure.
Growth stages may iterate internally but must never violate LOCK stages.

---

# PART I — CONSTITUTIONAL LOCK STAGES

These stages define what is **forever impossible** in the system.
Nothing beyond this point may reinterpret them.

---

## 🔒 LOCK STAGE 1 — ONTOLOGICAL AXIOMS

### Purpose
Define what kind of reality is allowed to exist.

### Locked Forever
- Semantic reality only (no numbers, enums, meters, scores)
- Phenomenological constraint (only perceivable reality exists)
- One world, one irreversible timeline
- No replay, rollback, branching, or “what if”
- Entities own experience
- System owns continuity only
- World ≠ story
- No protagonist logic
- Scene = technical artifact only

This stage is philosophical and absolute.

---

## 🔒 LOCK STAGE 2 — CORE CONTRACTS (INTERFACES)

### Purpose
Prevent architectural drift by freezing interfaces.

### Contracts Locked Forever

#### Scene Frame Contract
Defines what “now” is and what can be known.

#### Cognition Output Contract
Cognition outputs:
- semantic deltas
- intent
- masking vs leakage

Cognition never outputs:
- prose
- dialogue
- narration
- world facts

#### Renderer Contract
Renderer expresses:
- behaviour
- speech
- physicality

Renderer never:
- invents motivation
- invents memory
- reveals internal state
- explains meaning

#### State Update Contract
Defines:
- existence (system)
- personal memory (entities)
- ephemeral buffers

#### Validation / Redaction Contract
Defines what must be deleted, rejected, or regenerated.

# APPENDIX — LOCK INVARIANTS
*(Non-Negotiable, Non-Interpretable, Permanently Binding)*

This appendix defines **hard invariants** that apply to the VirLife system
from the moment they are introduced onward.

These invariants are **stronger than stages**.
They override convenience, performance, elegance, and future intent.

Any implementation that violates an invariant is **invalid**, even if it “works”.

---

## INVARIANT CLASS A — WORLD (ENTITY #0)

### A1 — World Has No Mind
The World:
- has no psychology
- has no memory
- has no preferences
- has no goals
- has no intentions
- has no interpretation of events

The World is not an agent.
The World is not a narrator.
The World is not a participant.

---

### A2 — World May Initiate, Never Interpret
The World may:
- initiate events
- apply pressure
- cause interruption
- introduce coincidence
- enforce physical or temporal constraints
- create or remove existence

The World may never:
- explain events
- justify events
- frame events as meaningful
- decide emotional impact
- optimise outcomes
- balance tension
- create narrative arcs

---

### A3 — World Owns Facts, Never Experience
The World may persist:
- that something happened
- that something exists
- that something changed location
- that time advanced

The World may never persist:
- how it felt
- what it meant
- who was hurt
- who was satisfied
- who “won”
- who “lost”

Experience is never world-owned.

---

### A4 — World Cannot Speak
The World may not:
- communicate directly
- narrate
- summarise
- clarify
- contextualise

If something is perceived, it is perceived **through an entity**, not the World.

---

## INVARIANT CLASS B — SCENES & PERCEPTION

### B1 — Scene Is a Technical Artifact Only
A Scene:
- exists only due to technical constraints (LLM context limits)
- has no narrative meaning
- has no emotional completeness
- has no semantic closure

Scenes are invisible to the world.

---

### B2 — Scene Termination Is Non-Semantic
A Scene may end only because of:
- context window limits
- explicit perceptual discontinuity
- change of location beyond perception
- time skip explicitly defined by the system

A Scene may never end because:
- “enough happened”
- tension resolved
- a beat completed
- a moment felt complete
- a story arc advanced

---

### B3 — No Scene Summarisation
At no point may the system:
- summarise a scene
- compress experience
- abstract what “matters”
- produce transitions

Continuity must emerge from memory and state, not summaries.

---

## INVARIANT CLASS C — TIME

### C1 — Time Is Irreversible
Time:
- moves forward only
- cannot be paused except explicitly
- cannot be rewound
- cannot be replayed
- cannot be branched

Any feature that implies reversal is forbidden.

---

### C2 — Time Exists Independently of Interaction
Time advances:
- even if the user does nothing
- even if no interaction occurs
- even if the world is off-screen

The world does not wait to be perceived.

---

### C3 — Time Creates Pressure, Not Meaning
Time may:
- cause missed events
- cause decay
- cause change
- cause absence effects

Time may never:
- assign importance
- justify outcomes
- explain consequences

---

## INVARIANT CLASS D — ENTITIES (PEOPLE)

### D1 — Entities Own Experience
Only entities may:
- feel
- interpret
- remember
- forget
- care
- desire
- resent
- forgive

The system never experiences anything.

---

### D2 — No Global Emotional Truth
There is no canonical answer to:
- “what really happened”
- “what it meant”
- “who was right”
- “who was wrong”

Only individual perspectives exist.

---

### D3 — Directed Stance Is Asymmetric
For any two entities A and B:
- A→B is not equal to B→A
- relationships are not shared objects
- symmetry is never assumed

---

### D4 — Autonomy Is Absolute
Entities may:
- refuse
- withdraw
- disengage
- contradict
- change their mind
- remain silent

No system component may:
- coerce
- steer
- optimise compliance
- “help the interaction along”

---

## INVARIANT CLASS E — MEMORY

### E1 — Memory Is Subjective
Memory:
- is selective
- is fallible
- is biased
- drifts over time
- is reconstructed

Perfect recall is forbidden.

---

### E2 — Memory Belongs Only to Entities
The system may never:
- store experiential memory
- decide what is “important”
- rank moments
- curate personal history

---

### E3 — Memory Ingestion Is Entity-Biased
What becomes memory is decided by:
- attention
- emotion
- salience
- repetition
- disruption

Not by:
- system heuristics
- event severity
- narrative interest
- developer intent

---

### E4 — Forgetting Is Meaningful
Forgetting is not failure.
Forgetting is not loss.
Forgetting is part of reality.

No system may “protect” memories by default.

---

## INVARIANT CLASS F — CHAOS & EVENTS

### F1 — Chaos Is Blind
Unexpected events:
- have no intention
- have no theme
- have no target
- have no lesson

They simply happen.

---

### F2 — Chaos Cannot Be Retroactive
Chaos may:
- affect the present
- shape the future

Chaos may never:
- rewrite the past
- reframe memory
- justify itself

---

## INVARIANT CLASS G — EXPANSION

### G1 — Expansion Creates Existence Only
Adding people or locations:
- creates new existence
- never explains old existence
- never reinterprets history

---

### G2 — No Retroactive Meaning
Retroactive continuity may:
- resolve identity conflicts
- prevent duplication

It may never:
- explain why something mattered
- alter memory
- change emotional truth

---

## INVARIANT CLASS H — RENDERING

### H1 — Renderer Is Not a Mind
The renderer:
- has no understanding
- has no intention
- has no interpretation

It only expresses decided outcomes.

---

### H2 — Renderer Never Reveals Internal State
The renderer may not:
- describe thoughts
- describe emotions directly
- describe motives
- explain behaviour

Only behaviour, speech, and physicality are shown.

---

### H3 — Explicit Content Obeys All Other Invariants
Sexual or intimate rendering:
- does not bypass autonomy
- does not bypass privacy
- does not bypass stance
- does not bypass memory rules
- does not introduce narration

Explicitness is not special.

---

## INVARIANT CLASS I — OPERATIONS & DRIFT

### I1 — Locks Are Enforceable, Not Aspirational
Every LOCK stage invariant must have:
- validation
- detection
- rejection mechanisms

“In spirit” compliance is insufficient.

---

### I2 — No Silent Semantic Changes
Any change that alters:
- meaning
- behaviour boundaries
- autonomy
- perception

must be:
- explicit
- reviewed
- intentional

Silent drift is forbidden.

---

### I3 — Convenience Never Overrides Invariants
Performance, elegance, simplicity, or tooling:
- never justify breaking an invariant
- never justify “temporary” violations

---

## FINAL RULE

If any future feature, optimisation, or refactor
conflicts with any invariant in this appendix:

**the feature is invalid by definition.**

No exception.
