## 0. Authority, Scope, and Precedence

### 0.1 Authority

This document defines the **governing laws of life** for the VirLife system.

These laws are **not implementation guidance**, **not mechanics**, and **not narrative principles**.
They are **non-negotiable constraints** that describe how life behaves, regardless of how it is implemented.

### 0.2 Scope

These laws apply to:
- all participants (including George),
- all time,
- all states,
- all interactions,
- all future extensions of the system.

They govern **lived human experience**, not physics, not sociology at scale, and not abstract simulation.

### 0.3 Precedence

If any other document conflicts with this one, this document **wins**.

## 1. The Nature of These Laws

These laws are:

- **Descriptive**, not aspirational  
- **Constraint-based**, not goal-based  
- **Continuous**, not event-triggered  
- **Invariant**, unless explicitly amended  

They describe what **must always be true** for life to remain recognisably human.

They do not guarantee:
- happiness
- harmony
- progress
- satisfaction
- clarity

They guarantee **realism**.

# THE LAWS

---

## LAW 1 — Temporal Irreversibility
### (“Time Is Irreversible and Accumulative”)

Time moves in one direction only.

Nothing resets.  
Nothing rewinds.  
Nothing jumps ahead.  
Nothing is undone.

All past states continue to exert influence on the present, either directly or indirectly.

There are no clean slates.
There are no retries.
There is no erasure of consequence.

Any system behaviour that:
- allows retroactive change,
- allows undoing lived experience,
- or allows skipping consequence,

**violates this law**.

### Addendum — Single World, No Relive

There exists exactly one world instance.

The system must not:
- create multiple worlds,
- fork timelines,
- allow “save slots”,
- allow “replay” or “relive” any prior moment as present.

History may be stored and consulted only as record and influence.
It must never become re-enterable state.

Any system behaviour that allows re-entering a past moment as the present **violates this LAW**.
---

## LAW 2 — State Precedes Event
### (“Life Exists Before Things Happen”)

At any moment, something is already true **before** anything happens.

Events do not create reality.
They only:
- reveal it,
- perturb it,
- or collide with it.

Life is not driven by happenings.
Life is driven by **what already exists underneath**.

Any system that:
- treats events as the primary drivers of life,
- or treats absence of events as absence of change,

**violates this law**.

---

## LAW 3 — Continuous Drift
### (“Change Is Continuous and Unavoidable”)

If time advances, state changes somewhere.

This change may be:
- small,
- invisible,
- slow,
- unsurfaced,
- or unnoticed.

Perfect stasis is artificial.

Change does not require:
- intention,
- action,
- acknowledgement,
- or narration.

Any system that allows time to pass with **no underlying change whatsoever** **violates this law**.

---

## LAW 4 — Limited Human Capacity
### (“Capacity Is Finite and Uneven”)

Every person has limited:
- attention,
- energy,
- emotional bandwidth,
- tolerance,
- willingness,
- availability.

These limits fluctuate over time.

No person can:
- respond to everything,
- care about everything,
- be available to everyone,
- or handle all pressures simultaneously.

Silence, delay, and non-response are **normal outcomes**, not failures.

Any system that assumes:
- constant availability,
- perfect responsiveness,
- or unlimited emotional capacity,

**violates this law**.

---

## LAW 5 — Accumulation Without Visibility
### (“Effects Accumulate Even When Unnoticed”)

Repeated exposure, behaviour, or absence produces change even when:
- it is not acknowledged,
- it is not noticed,
- it is not discussed,
- it is not remembered consciously.

Trust, resentment, safety, loyalty, erosion, and attachment are **cumulative phenomena**.

They do not require:
- dramatic moments,
- explicit recognition,
- or conscious accounting.

Any system that only allows change through explicit events **violates this law**.

---

## LAW 6 — Structural Conditions
### (“Some Realities Are Load-Bearing”)

Certain conditions, once present, exert **inescapable pressure** on life.

These conditions are:
- long-horizon,
- biologically or socially grounded,
- non-optional,
- and cross-domain.

Examples include (but are not limited to):
- pregnancy,
- serious illness,
- grief,
- long-term caregiving,
- public exposure,
- parenthood.

These are not events.
They are **structural loads**.

They propagate continuously across life until transformed or resolved.

Any system that treats such conditions as:
- single events,
- optional influences,
- or ignorable states,

**violates this law**.

---

## LAW 7 — Experiential Asymmetry
### (“Shared Life Does Not Mean Shared Experience”)

No two people experience the same situation in the same way.

The same reality can produce:
- different meanings,
- different impacts,
- different memories,
- different pressures,
- different priorities.

Relational life is inherently asymmetric.

Any system that assumes:
- mirrored experience,
- symmetrical impact,
- or shared meaning by default,

**violates this law**.

---

## LAW 8 — Crystallisation of Interaction
### (“What Works Becomes How Things Are Done”)

When a way of interacting:
- is perceived,
- produces predictable effects,
- and is not destructive,

it tends to stabilise into a pattern.

These patterns are not chosen.
They emerge.

Life runs largely on:
- habits,
- routines,
- norms,
- unspoken understandings.

Any system that requires explicit decision-making for every interaction **violates this law**.

---

## LAW 9 — Inertia of Established Patterns
### (“Patterns Persist Until Actively Broken”)

Once established, patterns persist by default.

They change only if:
- explicitly challenged,
- repeatedly contradicted,
- eroded by sustained pressure,
- or made impossible by life change.

Patterns do not decay simply because time passes.

Any system that lets established ways of relating fade arbitrarily **violates this law**.

---

## LAW 10 — Intentional Pressure
### (“Unresolved Intentions Create Force”)

Intentions create pressure even without action.

This includes:
- promises,
- plans,
- worries,
- desires,
- fears,
- unresolved decisions.

Pressure accumulates over time until:
- resolved,
- abandoned,
- transformed,
- or overridden.

Any system that treats intention as inert unless acted upon **violates this law**.

---

## LAW 11 — Non-Action Is Action
### (“Silence Has Consequences”)

Silence, delay, avoidance, and inaction are **real behaviours**.

They:
- affect others,
- shape relationships,
- generate interpretation,
- accumulate consequence.

Nothing is “neutral” simply because nothing was done.

Any system that treats non-action as absence of effect **violates this law**.

---

## LAW 12 — Private Meaning
### (“Meaning Exists Only Inside People”)

Meaning is subjective, private, and unverifiable.

People:
- misinterpret,
- project,
- misunderstand,
- hold contradictory beliefs,
- and never fully share internal meaning.

No system may:
- force shared understanding,
- enforce emotional clarity,
- or resolve ambiguity automatically.

Misunderstanding is a feature of life, not a bug.

---

## LAW 13 — Anti-Optimisation
### (“Life Is Not Efficient”)

Life does not optimise for:
- clarity,
- satisfaction,
- engagement,
- harmony,
- closure,
- efficiency.

Any optimisation of life experience produces distortion.

Systems must actively resist optimisation bias.

Any behaviour that makes life **easier than real life** without justification **violates this law**.

---

## LAW 14 — Opacity of Reality
### (“Most of Life Does Not Surface”)

Most changes:
- are invisible,
- are unspoken,
- are unacknowledged,
- are never narrated.

Only a small fraction of reality enters conscious experience.

Any system that surfaces too much, too often, **violates this law**.

---

## LAW 15 — Provenance
### (“Nothing Comes From Nowhere”)

Nothing appears without a plausible origin.

Origins may be:
- known,
- unknown,
- misunderstood,
- misattributed.

But they are never magical.

Any system that introduces state, events, or actors without plausible provenance **violates this law**.

---

## 16. Law Completeness Statement

This set of laws is considered **complete as a governing basis** for lived human experience within the system’s scope.

No new law may be introduced unless:
- a scenario cannot be explained as a consequence of these laws, **and**
- the gap cannot be resolved by refining mechanics or mappings.

These laws may change **only** through:
- explicit discussion,
- explicit versioning,
- explicit acceptance.
