# THE VIRLIFE ONTOLOGY
### Conceptual Data Model (Master v3.0)

**Version:** 3.0 (Unified)
**Status:** LOCKED 🔒
**Purpose:** To define the structure of existence for the VirLife Engine.
**Core Principle:** "Meaning over Mechanics." The system rejects rigid lists in favor of infinite semantic nuance.

---

## 1. CORE PHILOSOPHY

### 1.1 The Atomic Unit
The unit of the universe is the **Person (Entity)**.
Reality is not objective; it is a phenomenological experience filtered through the subjective senses and psychology of an observer.

### 1.2 The "No Enum" Rule
Life is not a list. We do not use fixed categories (e.g., "Friend," "Enemy") or numeric sliders (e.g., "Happiness: 80%"). We use **Semantic Description** (e.g., "A fragile truce held together by shared trauma").

### 1.3 The Agency of Fate (Entity #0)
The "World" is not a passive background. It is **Entity #0**—an active force of circumstance that uses the **Chaos Engine** to introduce entropy, surprise, and independent agency (The Initiator) to oppose the plans of the People.

## BUCKET 1: THE ENTITY (The Person)
*The atomic unit of experience and meaning.*

### 1.1 The Biological Core (The Hardware)
* **Hard Constraints:** Biological facts that restrict physical interaction.
    * *Examples:* Height, Build, Scent, Eye Color. (A 160cm person cannot physically block a 190cm person).
* **Tells (Unconscious):** Physical leakage of internal states.
    * *Example:* "Picks at cuticles when anxious."
* **Quirks (Conscious):** Specific preferences and sensory boundaries.
    * *Example:* "Hates the texture of wool," "Loves walking barefoot."

### 1.2 The Psychological Engine (The Software)
* **Psychological Mechanics:** Active instructions on *how* to process reality, not just a backstory.
    * *Example:* "Being interrupted triggers immediate withdrawal."
* **The Glass Box:** The Entity's internal monologue and secrets.
    * *Visibility:* Hidden from other Entities, but visible to the System (Renderer) to allow for dramatic irony and subtext.

### 1.3 The Intention & Goal Stack (Motivation)
Entities are not single-minded. They hold a **Dynamic List** of conflicting desires simultaneously.
* **Short Term (Impulses):** "Escape this awkward conversation."
* **Long Term (Drives):** "Preserve the friendship."
* **Result:** Behavior emerges from the tension between these conflicting intentions.

### 1.4 The Narrative Self
* **Personal Arcs:** The Entity's subjective interpretation of their own life story.
    * *Example:* "I am currently in my Redemption Arc."

## BUCKET 2: THE WEB OF STANCES (Interpersonal Reality)
*The asymmetric lens and social contracts.*

### 2.1 The Directed Bond (Asymmetry)
Relationships are not shared connections; they are individual perception filters.
* **The Two-Movie Theory:** `Entity A -> Entity B` is a completely separate record from `Entity B -> Entity A`.
* **Polyphony:** A relationship is a **Role Cluster**—a chord of multiple active roles playing simultaneously.
    * *Example:* George is "Neighbor" + "Rival" + "Confidant." The context determines which role is dominant.

### 2.2 The Promise Ledger (Social Contracts)
A specific, temporary structure tracking commitments.
* **Structure:** `Source` owes `Action` to `Target` by `Time`.
* **Consequence:** Unfulfilled promises generate "Tension" in the semantic texture of the relationship.

### 2.3 Semantic Texture
The "Vibe" of the relationship described in natural language.
* *Dimensions:* Trust, Intimacy, Power Dynamics, Sexual Tension.
* *Format:* Textual descriptions, not numbers.

## BUCKET 3: THE WORLD (Fate & Space)
*The active environment, timeline, and population.*

### 3.1 The Physical Map (Nesting)
* **Semantic Locations:** Places defined by atmosphere, not type.
    * *Example:* "The Blue Note: Smoky, loud, smells of stale beer."
* **Nesting Logic:** Entities exist within a hierarchy of containers.
    * `Room` $\subset$ `Building` $\subset$ `District` $\subset$ `Region`.
* **Expansion:** The system "hallucinates" (generates) new locations instantly when a user navigates to an undefined area.

### 3.2 The Timeline (The Future)
* **The Future Ledger:** A repository for **Calendar Entries**—events that are scheduled but have not yet occurred.
* **Function:** Allows Entities to plan ("See you Tuesday").
* **Trigger:** When Global Time matches Entry Time, the system spawns an Active Event.

### 3.3 Narrative Physics (Global Stories)
* **Global Arcs:** Long-running organising constructs that bind multiple people and events together.
    * *Example:* "The Corruption Scandal," "The Storm."
* **Director Mode:** The World Engine actively introduces obstacles to advance these arcs.

### 3.4 The Population Circles
The world is populated by three distinct tiers of agents to balance depth with scale.
1.  **The Inner Circle:** Fully simulated Entities with deep memory and subconscious processing (e.g., Rebecca).
2.  **The Cast:** Persistent Entities with names/jobs, but "frozen" when not present (e.g., The Landlord).
3.  **The Extras:** Ephemeral text-descriptions that vanish after the scene (e.g., The Crowd, The Barista).
    * *Mobility:* Extras can be promoted to Cast if they become significant (e.g., named).

## BUCKET 4: THE MEMORY MATRIX (The Metabolism)
*The subjective digestion of time.*

### 4.1 The Rashomon Storage
* **Subjectivity:** The system stores distinct memory traces for every participant of the same Event.
* **Conflict:** Entity A remembers "A Fight"; Entity B remembers "A Debate."

### 4.2 The Dual-Track System
* **Track A (Fact Ledger):** Instant, perfect recall of objective truths and semantic knowledge.
    * *Example:* "Paris is in France," "George hates cheese."
* **Track B (Experience Store):** Episodic memories that are compressed and distorted by emotion over time.
    * *Example:* "We had a wonderful night" (even if the transcript shows minor arguments).

### 4.3 The Retrieval Filter
* **Recall Probability:** Memory access is a spotlight, not a search bar.
* **Context Sensitivity:** Current Mood, Location, and Company determine which memories are accessible. (e.g., Sadness makes sad memories easier to recall).
