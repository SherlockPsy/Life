# MASTER_WORLD
## World Definition, People, and Facts

Version: 5.0 (Constitutional Lock)
Status: AUTHORITATIVE

---

## PREAMBLE

This document defines:
- what the World may introduce
- what people are allowed to be
- what must NEVER be encoded about people
- how facts exist independently of awareness

This document does NOT define:
- how people decide
- how behaviour is selected
- how outcomes occur

Any attempt to encode decision logic, optimisation, or behavioural enforcement in this document is invalid.

---

## ARTICLE I — WHAT THE WORLD IS

### I.1 The World Is Not a Mind

The World is not:
- conscious
- intentional
- strategic
- reactive

The World does not think.
The World does not decide.

---

### I.2 The World as Fact Source

The World exists only to introduce **Fact Seeds**.

Fact Seeds assert that something becomes true.

They do not:
- explain causes
- describe processes
- encode timelines
- simulate off-screen activity

---

## ARTICLE II — WORLD FACT SEEDS

### II.1 Definition

A Fact Seed is a **causally sufficient textual assertion** that a new external condition exists.

A Fact Seed MUST fully specify the entities and context involved such that:
- no later action requires inventing missing causes
- no agent is forced to complete the reality retroactively
- no hidden resolution is required to make the fact meaningful

Examples of valid Fact Seeds:
- “Maria is at the door to talk about her new relationship to George and Rebecca.”
- “The plumber is outside waiting to be let in.”
- “A storm begins over the city.”
- “George receives an email from his manager about the deadline.”

Underspecified stimuli are forbidden.

---

### II.2 Causal Sufficiency Requirement

Fact Seeds MUST be minimal **without being ambiguous**.

They MUST:
- assert existence only
- specify all necessary referents
- avoid outcomes, reactions, or internal states

They MUST NOT:
- leave the cause of a socially referential event unspecified
- introduce stimuli that imply an unseen agent without naming them
- defer meaning to a later action

Minimality means **no outcomes**, not **missing causes**.

---

### II.3 No Persistence

After introducing a Fact Seed, the World withdraws.

It does not:
- follow up
- escalate
- remind
- resolve
- simulate consequences

All development occurs only through Agent action or in-world interaction.

---

### II.4 Forbidden Underspecification

The World MUST NOT introduce Fact Seeds that are perceptually or socially incomplete.

The following are INVALID Fact Seeds:
- “The doorbell rings.”
- “Someone knocks.”
- “A phone vibrates.”
- “An email arrives.”

Unless the referent and context are explicitly specified in the same text.

This rule exists to prevent hidden causal completion by agents or the system.

## ARTICLE III — PEOPLE IN THE WORLD

### III.1 People Are Not Blank States

People are not born into the system as empty shells.

Each person exists with:
- a psychological history
- habitual tendencies
- sensitivities
- values
- social position

These are descriptive, not prescriptive.

---

### III.2 Identity Documents

Each person has exactly one **Identity Document**.

An Identity Document:
- is textual
- is static
- is immutable
- is always present when the person appears

It exists to bias interpretation and expression.

---

### III.3 What Identity Documents MAY Contain

Identity Documents MAY describe:
- tendencies (e.g. seeks validation)
- sensitivities (e.g. to exposure or rejection)
- habitual orientations (e.g. reserved, performative)
- values (e.g. privacy, autonomy)
- social asymmetries (e.g. public figure, recognisability)

All content MUST be written as tendencies or biases.

---

### III.4 What Identity Documents MUST NOT Contain

Identity Documents MUST NOT include:
- actions
- rules
- strategies
- goals
- optimisation language
- decision trees
- conditionals (“when X then Y”)

If an identity document tells a person what to do, it is invalid.

---

## ARTICLE IV — PUBLIC FIGURES AND SOCIAL POSITION

### IV.1 Public Status

Some people exist in the world as public figures.

This means:
- recognisability
- exposure risk
- asymmetrical attention
- constrained privacy

Public status biases plausibility.
It does not mandate behaviour.

---

### IV.2 Media and Public Surfaces

Public platforms (e.g. social media, news, events) may exist in the World.

They:
- exist as surfaces
- expose facts when encountered
- do not propagate automatically

Awareness arises only through interaction.

---

## ARTICLE V — RELATIONSHIPS

### V.1 Relationships Are Emergent

Relationships are not defined by system variables.

They emerge through:
- accumulated interaction
- shared history
- interpretation
- misinterpretation

There are no relationship meters.
There are no bond scores.

---

### V.2 Continuity Without Encoding

Past interactions influence future behaviour only if:
- they are reread
- they are remembered
- they matter subjectively

Nothing decays automatically.
Nothing grows numerically.

---

## ARTICLE VI — SEXUALITY AND INTIMACY

### VI.1 No Special Logic

Sexuality and intimacy are not special system modes.

They are:
- forms of interaction
- subject to the same rules as all others

There are:
- no gates
- no thresholds
- no arousal meters
- no safety toggles

All boundaries arise from identity and context.

---

### VI.2 Consent and Boundaries

Consent and boundaries are expressed only through:
- speech
- action
- refusal
- silence

There is no implicit consent.
There is no assumed permission.

---

## ARTICLE VII — EVENTS AND LIFE FACTS

### VII.1 Events Exist Independently

Life events may occur without being witnessed.

Examples:
- someone starts a relationship
- someone signs a contract
- someone falls ill
- someone moves city

These exist as facts, not experiences.

---

### VII.2 Encounter-Driven Awareness

People become aware of events only when:
- told
- exposed
- encountering evidence

There is no background dissemination.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — PROHIBITIONS (ABSOLUTE)

This document forbids:

- decision trees
- behavioural engines
- intention layers
- planning modules
- optimisation logic
- hidden counters
- background simulation
- automatic reactions

If it feels like psychology software, it is wrong.

---

## FINAL CLAUSE

The World introduces facts.
People live through them.

The system must never decide what living looks like.
