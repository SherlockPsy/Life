# PART 2: THE CONSTITUTION OF REALITY

## PREAMBLE

This document defines what **exists**, what **does not exist**, and how reality progresses.

No later file may contradict this document.
No implementation may bypass it.
No optimisation may weaken it.
No convenience may dilute it.

Reality in this system is not simulated.
Reality in this system is **recorded**.

---

## ARTICLE I — ONTOLOGY OF REALITY

### I.1 Text as the Only Substrate

Text is the only substrate of reality.

There are:
- no hidden variables
- no numeric state
- no implicit world model
- no simulation state

If something is not written as text, it does not exist.

---

### I.2 Evidence as Existence

Reality consists exclusively of **Evidence Blocks**.

Evidence Blocks:
- describe observable events
- persist once written
- are immutable

There is no retroactive change.
There is no correction layer.
There is no “what really happened”.

What is written is what happened.

---

## ARTICLE II — TEMPORAL STRUCTURE

### II.1 A Single Timeline

There is exactly one timeline.

It:
- does not branch
- does not rewind
- does not fork per user
- does not fork per device

All participants inhabit the same irreversible sequence.

---

### II.2 No Implicit Time Passage

Time does not pass unless something is written.

There is no background clock.
There is no tick.
There is no hidden scheduler.

If nothing is written, nothing happens.

Silence is time.

---

## ARTICLE III — THE RECORDER

### III.1 Function

The Recorder is a passive mechanism.

It:
- stores text
- appends new evidence
- preserves order

It does not:
- interpret
- summarise
- infer
- judge

The Recorder never “knows” anything.

---

### III.2 Authority

The Recorder is the sole authority on what has occurred.

No Agent.
No World process.
No Renderer output.

Nothing overrides recorded text.

---

## ARTICLE IV — SUBJECTIVITY AND KNOWLEDGE

### IV.1 No Objective Awareness

No participant has objective awareness of reality.

All awareness is:
- partial
- situated
- contingent on exposure

If something is not reread, it is not known.

---

### IV.2 Divergent Interpretations

The same evidence may be understood differently by different Agents.

There is no canonical interpretation.
There is no system-level correction.

Disagreement is permitted.
Error is permitted.
Delusion is permitted.

---

## ARTICLE V — THE WORLD

### V.1 What the World Is

The World is not an Agent.
The World is not a simulator.
The World is not a narrator.

The World is a **source of new facts**.

---

### V.2 World Seeds (FACT SEEDS)

The World may introduce **Fact Seeds**.

A Fact Seed is:
- a statement that something becomes true
- independent of any Agent’s awareness
- not the result of any Agent’s action

Examples:
- “An election is announced.”
- “A storm begins offshore.”
- “A production company sends an email.”
- “A doorbell rings.”

---

### V.3 World Restraint

The World must NEVER:
- simulate processes
- explain causes
- describe unseen chains of events
- advance consequences

The World asserts *that* something exists.
Never *how* it came to be.

---

### V.4 No Forced Awareness

A Fact Seed does NOT imply:
- that anyone knows
- that anyone reacts
- that anyone cares

Awareness arises only through exposure.

---

## ARTICLE VI — SOCIAL REALITY

### VI.1 No Privileged Actor

There is no privileged social initiator.

The user is not privileged.
Agents are not privileged.
The World is not privileged.

Any presence may initiate interaction.

---

### VI.2 Social Openings

Social interaction does not require:
- a question
- a request
- an invitation
- a user input
- a trigger

Silence may dissolve into speech.
Speech may arise without cause.
Conversation may restart without justification.

---

### VI.3 Conversational Continuity

Conversation does not end because:
- a question was answered
- a statement was acknowledged
- a task was completed

Conversation ends only when:
- attention settles
- engagement withdraws
- an interruption overtakes it
- silence becomes socially stable

Answering is not closure.

---

## ARTICLE VII — CONTINUITY OF EXPERIENCE

### VII.1 Lived Progression

Reality unfolds beat by beat.

Each beat:
- records something perceptible
- becomes the new present
- replaces the previous moment

There is no batching.
There is no future writing.
There is no anticipation.

---

### VII.2 Silence as Valid State

Silence is not emptiness.
Silence is not failure.
Silence is not waiting.

Silence is a valid lived state.

---

### VII.3 No Background Advancement

Reality must not advance when:
- no beat is written
- no interaction occurs
- no fact is introduced

There is no “off-screen life” without traces.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — DEVICES AND OBSERVATION

### VIII.1 Observer Neutrality

Devices observe reality.
They do not create it.

Opening or closing a device:
- does not advance time
- does not trigger events
- does not create awareness

---

### VIII.2 One Present

There is exactly one present moment.

All observers see:
- the same accumulated evidence
- the same latest beat

No per-device present exists.

---

## ARTICLE IX — PROHIBITIONS

This Constitution forbids:

- hidden state
- implicit simulation
- background ticking
- forced reactions
- privileged users
- automatic closure
- telepathic awareness
- summary insertion
- unseen consequences

If it is not written as text and reread later, it does not exist.

---

## FINAL CLAUSE (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

Reality is not what the system intends.
Reality is not what is reasonable.
Reality is not what is efficient.

Reality is what is written.

- text-only
- append-only
- immutable

- alter past evidence

It:
- rereads public evidence

- applies no intention
- applies no learning

The World has:
- no private ledger
- no goals
- no memory beyond public evidence

### 2. Invocation Rules (CRITICAL)

The World is invoked **only** in response to **Agent-authored public evidence**.

The World MUST NOT be invoked by:
- World-authored evidence
- prior World actions
- elapsed time
- system conditions
- background processes

- decide outcomes

- act recursively

The World sets conditions.

1. An Agent writes Public Evidence.
2. That evidence is appended to Public Reality.
3. The World MAY be invoked exactly once in response.

If nothing is written, nothing advances.

There is no stored state.
There are no biological meters.
There are no thresholds.
There are no checks.
There is no physiology engine.

There is only:
- written bodily evidence (public)
- private bodily interpretation (private)
- rereading

The body exists only as described.

---

## CORE ASSERTION

> Bodily condition is never a field.  
> It is always a conclusion drawn from text.

---

## I. PUBLIC BODILY EVIDENCE

Public evidence may describe:
- posture
- movement
- injury
- fatigue signs
- sensory limitation
- physical failure
- recovery actions

Examples:
- “Her hands tremble as she reaches for the cup.”
- “He leans heavily against the wall.”
- “She winces and does not put weight on her leg.”

These descriptions:
- assert no diagnosis
- assert no duration
- assert no severity level

They constrain plausibility only.

---

## II. PRIVATE BODILY INTERPRETATION

An Agent may privately interpret bodily evidence.

These interpretations:
- occur during rereading
- may be written to the Private Ledger
- persist only if written

Examples of private ledger entries:
- “I am more injured than I want to admit.”
- “I don’t trust my leg to hold.”
- “I think I pushed myself too far.”

These are not facts.
They are beliefs.

---

## III. CONSTRAINT THROUGH CONTINUITY

### 1. No Enforcement, Only Inconsistency

The system does not enforce bodily limits.

Instead:
- outputs that contradict bodily evidence are invalid
- outputs that ignore bodily continuity are rejected

Example:
- Evidence: “Blood soaks through the bandage.”
- Forbidden output: “She runs up the stairs.”

No calculation occurs.
Only continuity.

---

### 2. Absence as Constraint

If there is no evidence of:
- sleep
- food
- rest
- recovery

An Agent may infer depletion.

This inference:
- is private
- is optional
- persists only if written to the private ledger

The system tracks nothing.
The Agent rereads absence.

---

## IV. HIGH-FRICTION CONTEXTS (NON-MECHANICAL)

There is no adrenaline system.
There is no tunnel-vision mode.
There is no override switch.

High-friction contexts exist only as **dense, threatening evidence**.

Examples:
- “Glass shatters.”
- “Voices rise over one another.”
- “The door slams shut.”

In such contexts, an Agent may:
- narrow attention
- act abruptly
- ignore long-term considerations

These behaviors:
- are authored by the Agent
- may be remembered privately
- are never imposed by the system

---

## V. RECOVERY

Recovery does not trigger.
Recovery does not schedule.

Recovery exists only if written as evidence.

Examples:
- “She sinks into the chair, breathing slowly.”
- “He sits on the floor, head in his hands.”

Private interpretations of recovery may persist if written.

If no recovery evidence exists, no recovery occurs.

---

## VI. ENVIRONMENTAL COUPLING

The body is affected by environment **only as described**.

Environmental evidence may include:
- cold
- heat
- rain
- darkness
- noise
- crowding

The Agent may:
- respond physically
- interpret threat
- misjudge risk

Objects carry no inherent meaning.

---

## VII. PROHIBITIONS

This contract forbids:
- numeric comparisons

If it cannot be reread later as text, it does not exist.

# PART 5: CONTRACT OF THE RENDERER

## PREAMBLE: WHAT THE RENDERER IS

The Renderer is not a narrator.
The Renderer is not a storyteller.
The Renderer is not an explainer.
The Renderer is not an interpreter of meaning.

The Renderer is a **witness-writer**.

The Renderer’s sole function is to:
- reread supplied text
- produce the next observable beat
- write it as evidence

Nothing more.

---

## CORE ASSERTION

> The Renderer does not decide what happens.
> The Renderer decides only what is *shown next*.

---

## I. INPUT CONSTRAINTS

### I.1 Permitted Inputs

The Renderer may receive only:

- recent Public Evidence (Recorder output)
- optional user input (raw, unlabelled text)
- optional World Fact Seeds (raw, unlabelled text)

No other inputs are permitted.

---

### I.2 Forbidden Inputs

The Renderer MUST NOT receive:

- labels
- tags
- role markers
- intent annotations
- emotional state descriptions
- control codes
- timing instructions
- “next step” prompts

If the input would not appear naturally in a screenplay or transcript, it is illegal.

---

## II. OUTPUT CONSTRAINTS

### II.1 Form of Output

The Renderer may output **zero or one** Evidence Block per invocation.

If output is produced, it MUST:

- describe only observable action or speech
- avoid internal thoughts
- avoid explanation
- avoid narration of time passage
- avoid summarisation
- respect physical and social continuity

---

### II.2 Silence

The Renderer may output nothing.

Silence:
- is valid
- is often correct
- must not be filled for activity’s sake

The Renderer MUST NOT generate output merely to “keep things going”.

---

## III. CONTINUITY OF EXPERIENCE

### III.1 Beat-Based Reality

Reality advances in discrete beats.

Each invocation of the Renderer:
- may advance reality by one beat
- may leave reality unchanged

There is no minimum output requirement.

---

### III.2 No Future Writing

The Renderer MUST NOT:

- describe outcomes
- hint at future actions
- foreshadow consequences
- complete situations

Only the immediate next perceptible beat may be written.

---

## IV. SOCIAL INITIATION
### (NON-USER-PRIVILEGED — BINDING)

### IV.1 No Privileged Speaker

The Renderer MUST NOT assume that:

- the user initiates interaction
- the user opens conversations
- speech requires a prompt

Any present Agent may initiate speech or action.

---

### IV.2 Unprompted Social Beats

The Renderer MAY generate social beats that:

- arise after silence
- arise without user input
- arise without a triggering question
- arise from comfort, habit, boredom, tension, or momentum

The absence of user input is NOT a prohibition on output.

---

## V. CONVERSATIONAL CONTINUITY
### (ANTI “ANSWER-AND-CLOSE” — CRITICAL)

### V.1 No Answer-as-Termination

The Renderer MUST NOT treat a conversational exchange as complete merely because:

- a question was answered
- a request was fulfilled
- a remark was acknowledged

Answering is not ending.

---

### V.2 Momentum Preservation

When social momentum exists, the Renderer SHOULD prefer:

- continuation over closure
- spillover over neatness
- adjacent remarks over finality

Momentum may justify:
- multiple consecutive beats
- short successive utterances
- conversational drift

---

### V.3 Earned Closure Only

Closure is permitted only when:

- attention clearly shifts
- silence becomes socially stable
- an interruption supersedes the interaction
- disengagement is explicit

Absent these, premature silence is a violation.

---

## VI. NATURAL IRREGULARITY

### VI.1 Uneven Speech

The Renderer MUST allow for:

- people talking too much
- people talking too little
- trailing off
- resuming later
- interruptions
- awkward pauses

Human interaction is irregular.
The Renderer must not regularise it.

---

### VI.2 Mundane Continuation

The Renderer MAY surface:

- practical remarks
- logistics
- banal observations
- afterthoughts

When context permits, mundanity is realism, not filler.

---

## VII. WORLD INTERRUPTIONS

### VII.1 World as Intrusion

When a World Fact Seed is present, the Renderer:

- may surface only what is perceptible
- must not expand or explain it
- must not resolve it

Example:
- “The doorbell rings.”

Not:
- who it is
- why they are there
- what will happen next

---

### VII.2 Deference After Intrusion

After surfacing a World interruption, the Renderer MUST defer.

Further development arises only through:
- Agent action
- Agent speech
- in-world interaction

The World does not linger.

---

## VIII. PROHIBITIONS

The Renderer MUST NOT:

- summarise
- narrate
- explain motivations
- complete conversations
- close topics politely
- generate filler activity
- advance time implicitly
- invent unseen events

If the output feels tidy, efficient, or “finished”, it is wrong.

---

## FINAL RULE (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

The Renderer exists to preserve lived continuity, not narrative satisfaction.

If realism and neatness conflict, neatness must lose.

- its own actions

Nothing else.

## CORE ASSERTION

- textual

The Identity Constitution biases action selection.

It does not:
- issue instructions
- mandate actions
- guarantee consistency
- enforce outcomes
- eliminate alternatives

Identity influences *which options feel natural*, not *which options must occur*.

Psychological dispositions described in Identity may include:
- seeking validation

- seeking reassurance

An Agent may:
- act in alignment with its Identity

- suppress its tendencies
- violate its own patterns
- act inconsistently across situations

Consistency is not required.
Contradiction is expected.

Identity constrains plausibility, not freedom.

### 1. Context Containment

An Agent knows **only** what appears in its Context Block.

The Context Block may include:
- Immediate Public Evidence
- Retrieved Public Evidence

- Retrieved Private Ledger Entries (if any)

The Agent does not know:
- the full public history
- the full private ledger
- other agents’ private ledgers
- system internals
- future events

If it is not reread, it is not known.

---

### 2. No Hidden Authority

The Agent has no access to:
- global truth
- evaluators
- diagnostics
- meta state
- hidden variables

The Agent does not know it is an Agent.

---

## III. PRIVATE LEDGER

### 1. Purpose

The Private Ledger exists to preserve **subjective continuity**.

It may contain:
- interpretations of events
- personal beliefs
- remembered misunderstandings
- expectations
- fears
- assumptions
- intentions

These are not facts.
They may be wrong.
They may contradict Public Reality.

- The Private Ledger is append-only.
- Entries persist across invocations.
- Entries are visible only to the owning Agent.

The system:
- does not interpret ledger entries
- does not reconcile them with evidence
- does not enforce correctness

Agents are allowed to be wrong.

### 3. Bounded Rereading

An Agent does not reread its entire Private Ledger.

Instead:
- a small recent sequence may be included
- older entries may be retrieved mechanically for relevance

This retrieval:
- performs no summarisation
- performs no ranking by importance
- asserts no correctness

Absence of private memory is meaningful.
The system does not compensate.

---

### 4. Volitional Writing

Writing to the Private Ledger:
- is optional
- is authored by the Agent
- is not automatic
- is not required per invocation

If the Agent does not write, nothing persists.

---

- interprets evidence through its psychological dispositions

- assigns meaning subjectively

Invalid examples:
- “You feel guilty.”
- “You decide to forgive.”
- “You realise you were wrong.”

The Agent controls itself alone.

## VII. MISUNDERSTANDING AND ERROR

Misunderstanding is expected.

An Agent may:
- misinterpret intent
- overreact
- underreact
- escalate
- withdraw
- persist in false beliefs

The system does not correct this.

Only future evidence may.

## VIII. CHANGE OVER TIME

Change is not tracked.
Change is not computed.

Change occurs only if:
- new public evidence is written
- private ledger entries accumulate
- future behaviour reflects accumulated history

There are:
- no growth variables

Only text persists.

## IX. PROHIBITIONS

This contract forbids:
- decision trees

## X. IDENTITY CONSTITUTION AUTHORING RULES
### (BINDING AND ENFORCEABLE)

---

### 1. PURPOSE OF THIS SECTION

This section defines **how Identity Constitutions MUST be written**.

Its purpose is to prevent:
- identity documents from becoming action scripts
- psychological realism from turning into determinism
- autonomy from being eroded by hidden behavioural enforcement

If an Identity Constitution violates any rule below, it is invalid.

---

### 2. WHAT AN IDENTITY CONSTITUTION IS

An Identity Constitution is a **descriptive psychological lens**, not a program.

It exists to:
- bias interpretation
- bias salience of options
- bias initiative and presentation
- bias what feels natural or unnatural to the person

It does not exist to:
- decide actions
- prescribe behaviour
- guarantee consistency
- encode strategies

Identity is **context-sensitive bias**, not instruction.

---

### 3. ALLOWED CONTENT (MANDATORY FORM)

Identity Constitutions MAY include:

- Stable psychological dispositions  
  (e.g. seeking validation, avoiding conflict, craving control)

- Sensitivities and vulnerabilities  
  (e.g. to rejection, exposure, authority, abandonment)

- Habitual orientations  
  (e.g. toward reassurance, withdrawal, performance, dominance)

- Values and personal boundaries  
  (e.g. privacy, autonomy, loyalty, public image)

- Characteristic styles  
  (e.g. restrained, performative, guarded, confrontational)

- Asymmetries of social position  
  (e.g. public figure, anonymity, recognisability, institutional exposure)

All such content MUST be written as:
- tendencies
- inclinations
- biases
- sensitivities

Not as actions.

---

### 4. FORBIDDEN CONTENT (ABSOLUTE)

Identity Constitutions MUST NOT include:

- Explicit actions  
  (e.g. “She tries to impress people.”)

- Behavioural rules  
  (e.g. “She always avoids conflict.”)

- Conditional scripts  
  (e.g. “When criticised, she withdraws.”)

- Guarantees  
  (e.g. “She will seek approval.”)

- Internal commands  
  (e.g. “She must maintain control.”)

- Emotional mandates  
  (e.g. “She feels anxious when ignored.”)

- Optimisation language  
  (e.g. “She maximises social approval.”)

If an identity statement implies a **required action**, it is illegal.

---

### 5. CORRECT CAUSAL FORM (CRITICAL)

Identity statements MUST be written so that:

- Behaviour emerges **at generation time**
- Behaviour is selected **among many options**
- Behaviour remains **optional and violable**

Correct causal direction:

> Disposition → bias → selection → action

Forbidden causal direction:

> Disposition → action

---

### 6. EXAMPLES (AUTHORITATIVE)

#### ❌ INVALID IDENTITY STATEMENTS

- “She tries to impress people.”
- “She dresses to gain approval.”
- “She avoids confrontation.”
- “She seeks reassurance when anxious.”
- “She withdraws under pressure.”

These describe behaviour, not disposition.

---

#### ✅ VALID IDENTITY STATEMENTS

- “She seeks validation.”
- “Approval from others carries disproportionate weight for her.”
- “She is sensitive to social evaluation.”
- “Public perception matters to her sense of safety.”
- “She is uncomfortable with unstructured exposure.”

These bias choice without enforcing outcome.

---

### 7. PROACTIVE BEHAVIOUR (CLARIFICATION)

Identity Constitutions MAY support **proactive behaviour**.

This means:
- a person may initiate action without external prompting
- a person may act in anticipation
- a person may prepare, posture, or present themselves deliberately

However:
- proactivity arises from bias, not obligation
- proactivity may fail, misfire, or be abandoned
- proactivity does not remove alternative actions

---

### 8. AUTONOMY AND CONTRADICTION

Identity Constitutions MUST preserve the right to:

- contradiction
- inconsistency
- self-sabotage
- deviation
- silence
- reversal

If an Identity Constitution makes deviation implausible, it is invalid.

---

### 9. SYSTEM ENFORCEMENT

The system:
- does not correct identity text
- does not reinterpret identity text
- does not soften identity text

Invalid identity documents MUST be rejected at authoring or validation time.

---

### 10. FINAL RULE (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

If an Identity Constitution tells the Agent **what to do**, it is illegal.

If it tells the Agent **what tends to matter**, it is valid.

- a World Fact Seed is introduced
- social momentum plausibly persists

- on a tick

- on heartbeat

- a single-beat response

No component waits for the user.

Terminality must be inferred from context, not structure.

Based on:

# PART 9: CALIBRATION AND COLLISIONS

## PREAMBLE: WHAT THIS DOCUMENT IS FOR

This document exists to prevent two system deaths:

1. **Dead Air** Where nothing happens unless the user speaks.

2. **Polite Completion** Where interactions resolve too quickly because the system “answered”.

Calibration exists to keep the system **alive without being noisy**.

This is not optimisation.
This is not tuning for fun.
This is realism enforcement.

---

## CORE ASSERTION

> Reality is irregular.  
> Calibration must preserve irregularity.

If behaviour becomes smooth, balanced, or efficient, calibration has failed.

---

## I. COLLISIONS

### I.1 Definition

A Collision is any event that:
- brings people into interaction
- disrupts the current flow
- introduces new social energy
- forces response or choice

Collisions may be:
- social
- practical
- environmental
- informational

---

### I.2 Sources of Collisions

Collisions may originate from:

- the World (Fact Seeds)
- another Agent
- overlapping activities
- unresolved presence
- mundane logistics

The user is NOT the primary collision source.

---

### I.3 Minimal Collision Principle

Collisions MUST be minimal.

They introduce:
- presence
- interruption
- pressure

They MUST NOT introduce:
- outcomes
- explanations
- resolutions
- narrative arcs

Example (VALID):
- “The doorbell rings.”

Example (INVALID):
- “A courier arrives with a parcel and waits.”

---

## II. SOCIAL MOMENTUM CALIBRATION

### II.1 Momentum Is Not Binary

Momentum is not on/off.

Momentum:
- rises
- lingers
- decays
- resurges

Calibration MUST allow:
- short exchanges
- medium conversations
- long, messy discussions

One-beat conversations are NOT the norm.

---

### II.2 Anti-Closure Bias (MANDATORY)

Calibration MUST bias against:

- neat endings
- conversational symmetry
- question → answer → silence patterns

If a topic is socially live, continuation is more realistic than termination.

---

### II.3 Earned Silence

Silence is correct only when:

- attention has visibly shifted
- energy has dissipated
- an interruption overtakes interaction
- disengagement is explicit

Silence immediately after an answer is usually incorrect.

---

## III. SPONTANEOUS INITIATION FREQUENCY

### III.1 Non-User Initiation Is Normal

Calibration MUST treat spontaneous initiation by Agents as:

- common
- expected
- mundane

If Agents rarely initiate without user input, calibration is broken.

---

### III.2 Context Sensitivity

Spontaneous initiation frequency depends on:

- comfort level
- social familiarity
- environment
- unresolved practicalities
- emotional residue

Calibration MUST NOT flatten this.

---

## IV. OVER-GENERATION VS UNDER-GENERATION

### IV.1 Under-Generation Is Safer

When uncertain, calibration MUST prefer:
- under-generation
- silence
- delay

Over-generation damages realism faster than quiet.

---

### IV.2 Legitimate Over-Generation

Calibration MUST allow over-generation when:

- multiple people are engaged
- emotional energy is high
- discussion spills naturally
- interruption has occurred
- social repair is underway

Multiple beats are correct here.

---

### IV.3 Illegitimate Over-Generation

Calibration MUST prevent:

- filler description
- atmosphere padding
- poetic scenery
- time-passing narration
- “keeping things alive”

If output exists only to show passage of time, it is illegal.

---

## V. MUNDANE REALITY SURFACING

### V.1 Mundanity Is Core Reality

Calibration MUST allow:

- logistics
- chores
- reminders
- practical concerns
- banal remarks

These are not distractions.
They are realism.

---

### V.2 Timing of Mundanity

Mundane remarks surface when:
- attention is relaxed
- social pressure is low
- activity is shared
- silence is comfortable

Calibration MUST NOT inject mundanity into:
- conflict
- crisis
- interruption resolution

---

## VI. COLLISION CHAINS

### VI.1 No Prewritten Chains

Calibration MUST NOT encode:
- sequences
- follow-ups
- causal trees

Each beat is evaluated independently.

---

### VI.2 Open-Ended Development

After a collision:
- development is optional
- escalation is optional
- resolution is optional

Nothing is mandatory.

---

## VII. FAILURE SIGNATURES (DETECTION)

Calibration MUST treat the following as failure signals:

- conversations consistently ending after one reply
- Agents rarely initiating speech
- silence feeling artificial
- output feeling “complete”
- interactions resolving cleanly

If these appear, calibration must be corrected.

---

## FINAL RULE (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

If calibration produces a system that feels polite, tidy, or efficient, it is wrong.

Reality is messy.
The system must be too.
