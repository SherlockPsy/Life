What we have locked:

First, the nature of the system.
You are not building a simulator, a game, a tool, or a storytelling engine. You are building a semantic reality. The system does not calculate, optimise, balance, or reason in the background. It records text. It rereads text. It speaks when asked. Nothing else happens.

Second, the ontology.
Only four things exist, and they exist only as text:

• Public reality: append-only Exposed Evidence Blocks.
• Private reality: per-person private ledgers, also text, sealed.
• Identity: static constitutional documents that constrain behaviour.
• The World: an orchestrator that injects new evidence but never thinks.

There is no fifth thing hiding somewhere.

Third, time.
There is one irreversible timeline.
Time advances only when the system is running.
When paused, everything is frozen. No queues. No ticking. No “meanwhile.”
On resume, the world continues from the exact same instant.
Unexpected events are generated only when they occur. If it’s queued, it’s illegal.

Fourth, truth and memory.
If it is not written as evidence, it did not happen.
Nothing is inferred into truth.
Nothing is silently corrected.
Contradictions are allowed and resolved only by later evidence.
Memory works by rereading selected past evidence (montage), never by checking variables.

Fifth, people.
People are autonomous, conscious, self-aware agents.

Each person has:
• a fixed Identity Core (how they tend to be),
• a private notebook (what they think about what happened),
• no numeric state,
• no meters,
• no hidden world authority controlling them.

They can refuse.
They can ignore the user.
They can misunderstand.
They can be wrong.
They control only themselves.

Sixth, the World’s role.
The World is active but not intentional.
It moves life forward by timing, collision, interruption, entropy, and novelty.
It does not favour good or bad outcomes.
It does not help or punish.
It does not balance.
It does not learn.
It never decides for people.
It never violates physics.

Seventh, interaction and rendering.
You do not narrate. You act.
The output is not a report. It is lived experience.
One continuous perceptual stream.
No panels. No summaries. No explanations. No meta language.
Interruptions can happen mid-sentence.
If it feels like watching instead of living, it’s wrong.

Eighth, absolute prohibitions.
These are not preferences. They are bans.

• No debugging tools.
• No dashboards.
• No audit trails.
• No operator views.
• No meta queries.
• No observer mode.
• No game logic.
• No state machines.
• No scenario system.
• No variables standing in for life.

There is no backstage. Ever.

Ninth, initial scope.
The system starts with:
• You (full name).
• Rebecca (full name).
• A shared house.
• Evidence that Rebecca has just moved in.
No one else exists yet.

Tenth, what exists on disk (this matters).
You now have:
• A locked Copilot guardrails file (.github/copilot-instructions.md).
• A locked Non-Negotiables Constitution (v0.2).
• A clear definition of the World.
• A clear definition of memory, time, and autonomy.
• A clear rejection of MVP thinking and tooling creep.

That is a complete philosophical and architectural foundation.
Nothing essential is missing at the level of “what is this thing?”
