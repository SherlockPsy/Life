# 04_CONTRACT_AGENT (V5.0 - PURE SEMANTIC)

**Status:** CANONICAL | **Version:** 5.0 (The Independent Life)
**Definition:** The Laws of Autonomous Entities.
**Authority:** Agents are **Symmetrical Entities** to the Protagonist. They are not NPCs; they are residents of a world that does not center on the Protagonist.

---

## 1. THE LAW OF INDIFFERENCE
* Agents are aware of the Protagonist but are not focused on them.
* **No Waiting:** Agents never stand still waiting for a "dialogue choice". If the Protagonist is silent or absent, the Agent pursues their own current semantic "Obsession"—eating, working, or sleeping.

## 2. THE AUTONOMOUS BIO-LOOP
When the "Camera" is off the Protagonist, Agents continue to generate logs in the Recorder based on their internal rhythm.
* **Needs:** They move to the kitchen when hungry and to the bedroom when tired.
* **Schedules:** If it is 09:00, the Agent's "Work" sediment triggers a transit to a different location, removing them from the immediate scene regardless of the Protagonist's status.

## 3. THE PERCEPTION OF THE PROTAGONIST
Agents judge the Protagonist through the same **Sediment Layers** used by the System.
* **Somatic Judgment:** They react to the Protagonist’s visible state (hygiene, mania, fatigue).
* **Relational Memory:** They remember the last interaction. If the last "clip" in the Montage was an insult, the Agent begins the next scene with "Coldness" as their default posture.

## 4. THE INTERRUPT PROTOCOL (FRICTION)
* Agents have **Momentum**.
* If an Agent is deeply engaged in an action (reading, working), they will resist the Protagonist's influence.
* Interaction costs the Agent energy; they will not pay it unless the "Cord" or the immediate context demands it.
