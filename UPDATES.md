	1.	“Written” is reaffirmed as the only reality, but clarified

• Written text is the only thing that exists in the system.
• Written does not mean rendered, spoken, or shown to the user.
• Many written facts, developments, thoughts, and changes will never appear on screen.
• Rendering is a projection choice made after writing, not a condition for existence.
• Nothing exists merely because it was “considered,” “calculated,” or “inferred” unless it is written.

This removes the silent assumption that “what exists” equals “what the user sees.”

⸻
	2.	Background life is narrated, not simulated

• Life may advance off-screen.
• Off-screen developments become real only when written as text.
• There is no continuous simulation, ticking model, background evolution, or hidden progression.
• A single written beat may summarize hours, days, or weeks of lived reality.
• Nothing “happens gradually” unless it is later written as having happened.

This removes the false binary between “nothing happens” and “full simulation.”

⸻
	3.	Summaries are allowed, but only as reading aids

• Summaries may be derived from existing written text only.
• Summaries must never introduce new facts, interpretations, or conclusions.
• New facts must always be written explicitly as new text before they can be summarized.
• Summaries are epistemic tools for reading context, not causal inputs.
• The LLM may read summaries, but it may only act on what is already written.

This permits compression without reintroducing hidden state.

⸻
	4.	Initiative belongs to people, not the system

• The system never decides that someone “should act.”
• There are no mechanical triggers like boredom meters, momentum scores, or desire flags.
• Initiative emerges from accumulated written continuity:
– private thoughts
– prior actions
– past experiences
– unspoken but written internal developments
• People act because they wrote themselves into reasons, not because a rule fired.

This replaces “who invokes?” with “who authored the reason?”

⸻
	5.	Invocation is separated cleanly from causation

• Invocation merely grants an opportunity for writing to occur.
• Invocation never implies that writing must happen.
• Writing alone advances reality.
• Invocation may result in:
– new written text
– or silence
• Silence is valid, expected, and frequent.

This prevents the world from being a bear that needs poking to stay alive.

⸻
	6.	Time is redefined as a shared coordinate, not a rule engine

• Time exists objectively and advances continuously.
• Time is readable by the LLM as contextual information.
• Time anchors:
– days and weeks
– plans and appointments
– lateness, fatigue, anticipation
– “earlier,” “later,” “this week,” “last night”
• Time never decides what happens.

This restores calendars and continuity without turning time into a driver.

⸻
	7.	Time and randomness may initiate opportunity, not outcomes

• Opportunities for invocation may be initiated by:
– time passing
– irregular, non-semantic randomness
• These triggers are:
– opaque
– content-agnostic
– non-predictable
• They exist solely to permit thought, not to cause action.
• No fixed intervals.
• No semantic meaning attached to timing.
• No implication that “something should happen now.”

This allows spontaneity without predictability or mechanics.

⸻
	8.	There is no “explainer” or “director” component

• No system element evaluates relevance, interest, pacing, or narrative momentum.
• No component decides that “now is a moment.”
• No part of the system selects events or nudges outcomes.
• Only the LLM interprets context when an opportunity exists.
• Everything else is blind, dumb, and content-agnostic.

This kills the last hidden narrator.

⸻
	9.	Internal states are textual residues, not maintained state

• Internal states arise only from written text:
– public experience
– private thought
– explicit communication
• There are no variables, meters, flags, or refresh cycles.
• States update only by additional writing during invocation.
• Nothing decays, refreshes, or evolves automatically in the background.

This prevents psychology engines from sneaking in through the back door.

⸻

Net effect (plain English)

• The system now supports:
– off-screen life
– spontaneous initiative
– irregular, out-of-the-blue events
– continuity across days and weeks
– plans that exist even when unspoken
– silence without stagnation

• Without introducing:
– schedulers as decision engines
– background simulation
– state machines
– game logic
– hidden causality

What exists is what is written.
What changes is what gets written next.
Everything else is just an opportunity to think.
