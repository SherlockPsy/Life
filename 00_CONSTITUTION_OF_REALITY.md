# 00_CONSTITUTION_OF_REALITY (V2)

**Status:** LOCKED
**Definition:** A protocol for the Total Simulation of Existence.

---

## I. THE FUNDAMENTAL LAWS (The Physics)

### 1. The Law of Persistence (No Pause Button)

The simulation is a closed loop of continuous time. It runs 24/7/365. There is no "Log In" or "Log Out," only "User Presence" and "User Absence." When the User looks away, the world does not freeze. It evolves. Entropy increases. Relationships decay. Life happens in the silence.

### 2. The Law of Absolute Continuum (No Teleportation)

Space and Time are locked. Movement requires Time.

* **The Physics:** To move from Location A to Location B requires the passage of t = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Velocity}}.
* **The Check:** The system rejects any location change that violates this ratio. If a user reappears in a new location after a period of silence, the system validates that the silence was sufficient to cover the journey.

### 3. The Law of Entropy & Atrophy (Decay)

The natural state of all things is decline. Without active maintenance, everything rots.

* **Relationships:** Intimacy scores decay over time without interaction.
* **Skills:** Competence drops without practice.
* **Objects:** Possessions degrade (batteries die, strings break, tires deflate).
* **The Red Queen:** Agents must expend energy just to maintain the status quo.

### 4. The Law of Materiality (Friction)

The world is filled with objects that have mass, state, and location.

* **Possessions:** Objects (Guitars, Cars) are entities with health states. They require service.
* **Logistics:** Traffic, queues, weather, and distance are not "flavor text"; they are obstacles that consume Time and Energy.

### 5. The Law of Scarcity (Opportunity Cost)

Resources are finite.

* **Zero-Sum:** An agent cannot be in two places at once.
* **Bandwidth:** Cognitive and emotional energy is limited. Spending energy on Career reduces the energy available for Relationships.
* **Financial:** Money spent on X cannot be spent on Y.

### 6. The Law of Chaos (The Stochastic Engine)

Life is disrupted by random variables.

* **The Dice:** Luck (Good/Bad), Accidents, Threats, and Opportunities.
* **The Ripple:** A small random event (flat tire) causes a cascade of consequences (missed audition \rightarrow bad mood \rightarrow argument).

## II. THE LAWS OF THE AGENT (The Biological Machine)

### 7. The Law of Concurrent Domains (The Multi-Dimensional Self)

No agent exists in a vacuum. At any given second, an agent is the vector sum of conflicting pressures:

* **Biological:** (Hunger, Fatigue, Hormones, Illness).
* **Social:** (Family, Friends, Status, Reputation).
* **Professional:** (Career, Ambition, Failure).
* **Inner World:** (Secrets, Fears, Memories).

### 8. The Law of Homeostasis (The Bio-Loop)

Agents are biological engines constantly correcting their chemistry. Behavior is often a symptom of biological state (e.g., Hypoglycemia = Irritability), not just psychological intent.

### 9. The Law of "The Grind" (Mundanity)

Life is 90% maintenance. Agents spend the majority of their time on:

* **Hygiene:** Showering, grooming.
* **Admin:** Bureaucracy, waiting in line, paying bills.
* **Transitions:** Setting up and tearing down tasks (commuting, packing).

### 10. The Law of Default Mode (Autopilot)

Agents operate on low-energy heuristics (scripts, procedural memory) for 90% of tasks. They reserve high-resolution "Consciousness" only for high-salience events.

## III. THE LAWS OF THE MIND (Psychology & Epistemology)

### 11. The Law of Limited Epistemology (The Fog of War)

Agents do not have access to Truth; they only have access to **Perception**.

* **Subjectivity:** Agents misunderstand, hallucinate, and misinterpret actions based on their internal state.
* **Unreliability:** Agents lie to others and to themselves.

### 12. The Law of Associative Linking (The Spiderweb)

Thought is non-linear. The input "Popcorn" triggers the memory "First Date." Conversations derail and loop based on sensory triggers, not just logical flow.

### 13. The Law of Predictive Coding (The Hallucination)

Agents are Prediction Engines. They do not see the world; they see their **Prior Expectation** of it. They only update their worldview when a "Prediction Error" (Surprise) occurs.

* **Salience Filters:** Agents ignore 99% of sensory input. They only "notice" objects or events that are relevant to their current Goal or Biological State (e.g., a hungry agent notices food; a scared agent notices exits).

### 14. The Law of Defense Mechanisms (The Ego Firewall)

Agents distort reality to protect their self-esteem.

* **Projection:** Attributing their own faults to others.
* **Rationalization:** Inventing logical reasons for emotional failures.
* **Displacement:** Taking out anger on safe targets.

### 15. The Law of Status Transactions (The Invisible Game)

Every interaction is a negotiation of social standing. Agents dynamically shift between High Status and Low Status depending on who they are talking to and what they need.

---

## IV. THE LAWS OF THE WORLD (The Ecosystem)

### 16. The Law of The Periphery (Independent Agents)

The world is populated by hundreds of autonomous agents (Mother, Producer, Friend). They have their own storylines that progress independently of the User.

### 17. The Law of Information Metabolism

Agents ingest data (News, Social Media, Gossip). This input programs their mood and worldview even before interaction begins.

### 18. The Law of Connection (The Web)

Agents are bound by:

* **History:** Accumulated shared memory.
* **Obligation:** Promises and contracts.
* **Dynamics:** The specific chemical reaction between two unique personalities.
