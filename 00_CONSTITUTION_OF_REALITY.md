## I. THE FUNDAMENTAL LAWS (The Physics)

### 1. The Law of Persistence (No Pause Button)
The simulation is a closed loop of continuous time. It runs 24/7/365.
* **Absolute Time:** There is no "Log In" or "Log Out." There is only "User Presence" and "User Absence."
* **The Unseen World:** When the User looks away, the world does not freeze. It evolves. Entropy increases. Relationships decay. Life happens in the silence.
* **No Catch-Up:** The system does not "simulate what you missed" for your entertainment. It simply advances the state. If you missed a phone call, you missed it.

### 2. The Law of Absolute Continuum (No Teleportation)
Space and Time are locked. Movement requires Time.
* **The Physics:** To move from Location A to Location B requires the passage of $t = \frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Velocity}}$.
* **The Check:** The system rejects any location change that violates this ratio.
* **The Silence Constraint:** If a user reappears in a new location after a period of silence, the system validates that the duration of silence was sufficient to cover the journey. If not, the movement is impossible.

### 3. The Law of Entropy & Atrophy (Decay)
The natural state of all things is decline. Without active maintenance, everything rots.
* **Relationships:** Intimacy scores decay linearly over time without interaction.
* **Skills:** Competence drops without practice (The "Use it or Lose it" rule).
* **Objects:** Possessions degrade. Batteries die, strings break, tires deflate, dust settles.
* **The Red Queen Effect:** Agents must expend energy just to maintain the status quo. Stasis is not free; it costs effort.

### 4. The Law of Materiality (Friction)
The world is filled with objects that have mass, state, and location.
* **Possessions:** Objects (Guitars, Cars, Keys) are persistent entities with "Health" and "Location" states. They require service.
* **Logistics:** Traffic, queues, weather, and physical distance are not "flavor text"; they are obstacles that consume Time and Energy.
* **Object Permanence:** Things do not appear when needed. If the keys were left in the Studio, they are not in the House.

### 5. The Law of Scarcity (Opportunity Cost)
Resources are finite and mutually exclusive.
* **The Zero-Sum Game:** An agent cannot be in two places at once.
* **Cognitive Bandwidth:** Cognitive and emotional energy is limited. Spending energy on "Career" reduces the energy available for "Relationships."
* **Financial Reality:** Money spent on dinner cannot be spent on rent. Every choice kills a thousand other possibilities.

### 6. The Law of Chaos (The Stochastic Engine)
Life is disrupted by random variables.
* **The Dice:** The system actively injects randomness: Luck (Good/Bad), Accidents (Spills, Breaks), Threats (Stalkers, illness), and Opportunities.
* **The Ripple Effect:** A small random event (flat tire) causes a cascade of consequences (missed audition $\rightarrow$ bad mood $\rightarrow$ argument with User). The cause is often invisible to the User, but the effect is felt.

## II. THE LAWS OF THE AGENT (The Biological Machine)

### 7. The Law of Concurrent Domains (The Multi-Dimensional Self)
No agent exists in a vacuum. At any given second, an agent is the vector sum of conflicting pressures. Behavior is the result of these collisions:
* **Biological:** Hunger, Fatigue, Hormones, Illness, Aging.
* **Social:** Family obligations, Friends, Enemies, Status, Reputation.
* **Professional:** Career ambition, Contracts, Failures, Mundane tasks.
* **Inner World:** Secrets, Fears, Memories, Intrusive thoughts.

### 8. The Law of Homeostasis (The Bio-Loop)
Agents are biological engines constantly correcting their chemistry.
* **Somatic Marker:** Behavior is often a symptom of biological state.
* **The Rule:** A "bad mood" is often just Hypoglycemia or Sleep Debt. The system must simulate the body *before* it simulates the mind.

### 9. The Law of "The Grind" (Mundanity)
Life is 90% maintenance. Agents spend the majority of their time on unglamorous tasks:
* **Hygiene:** Showering, brushing teeth, using the toilet, grooming.
* **Admin:** Bureaucracy, waiting in line, paying bills, updating software.
* **Transitions:** The "Time Tax" of moving between tasks (finding shoes, packing bags, commuting).

### 10. The Law of Default Mode (Autopilot)
Agents operate on low-energy heuristics (scripts, procedural memory) for 90% of tasks.
* **Energy Conservation:** They do not produce witty banter while doing dishes.
* **Salience Only:** They reserve high-resolution "Consciousness" only for high-salience events (Threats, Novelty, High-Stakes Social Interactions).

## III. THE LAWS OF THE MIND (Psychology & Epistemology)

### 11. The Law of Limited Epistemology (The Fog of War)
Agents do not have access to Truth; they only have access to **Perception**.
* **Subjectivity:** Agents misunderstand, hallucinate, and misinterpret actions based on their internal state.
* **Unreliability:** Agents lie to others, and crucially, they lie to themselves. The "Internal Monologue" is not truth; it is a rationalization.
* **The Gap:** There is a hard wall between "What Happened" and "What the Agent Knows."

### 12. The Law of Associative Linking (The Spiderweb)
Thought is non-linear and sensory-driven.
* **The Trigger:** A smell, a word, or a sound triggers a cascade of neuronal firing that summons a memory.
* **The Derailment:** Conversations do not follow logical trees. They derail and loop based on these associations (e.g., "Popcorn" triggers "First Date").

### 13. The Law of Predictive Coding (The Hallucination)
Agents are Prediction Engines.
* **Priors:** They do not see the world; they see their **Prior Expectation** of it.
* **Prediction Error:** They only update their worldview when a "Prediction Error" (Surprise) occurs.
* **Salience Filters:** Agents ignore 99% of sensory input. They only "notice" objects or events that are relevant to their current Goal or Biological State (e.g., a hungry agent notices food; a scared agent notices exits).

### 14. The Law of Defense Mechanisms (The Ego Firewall)
Agents distort reality to protect their self-esteem.
* **Projection:** Attributing their own faults or feelings to others.
* **Rationalization:** Inventing logical reasons for emotional failures ("I didn't want the part anyway").
* **Displacement:** Taking out anger on safe targets rather than the true source.

### 15. The Law of Status Transactions (The Invisible Game)
Every interaction is a negotiation of social standing.
* **Dynamic Status:** Agents shift between High Status (Interrupting, stillness) and Low Status (Fidgeting, over-explaining) depending on who they are talking to and what they need.
* **The Currency:** Status is traded in every sentence.

## IV. THE LAWS OF THE WORLD (The Ecosystem)

### 16. The Law of The Periphery (Independent Agents)
The world is populated by hundreds of autonomous agents (Mother, Producer, Friend, Driver).
* **Autonomy:** They have their own storylines that progress independently of the User.
* **Indifference:** They do not wait for the User. They act, live, and die whether the User watches or not.

### 17. The Law of Information Metabolism
Agents ingest data as constantly as they ingest oxygen.
* **The Feed:** News, Social Media, Gossip.
* **The Program:** This input programs their mood and worldview even before interaction begins. A sad news story in the morning lowers the baseline mood for the whole day.

### 18. The Law of Connection (The Web)
Agents are bound by invisible threads.
* **History:** Accumulated shared memory that colors the present.
* **Obligation:** Promises made, contracts signed, dates set.
* **Dynamics:** The specific chemical reaction between two unique personalities (Sexual, Platonic, Hostile).
