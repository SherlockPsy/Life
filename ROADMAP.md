LIFE SYSTEM — BINDING ROADMAP (INEVITABLE VERSION)

This document defines the only acceptable progression from an empty deployed system
to a production-ready continuous life.

Each milestone defines:
- what kinds of text may be written
- what kinds of text may be reread
- what kinds of invocations are possible
- what must be observably true once the milestone is complete

No milestone introduces background activity, hidden state, simulation,
or system-level decision-making.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––
MILESTONE 0 — THE WORLD CAN EXIST
––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

Purpose:
A deployed place exists where text can be written and read, without implying life yet.

What becomes possible:
- The system can be invoked via HTTP.
- The system can confirm that it is alive enough to accept text later.

What is written:
- Nothing about people.
- Nothing about the world.
- No history.

Observable truths:
- An invocation such as `GET /health` returns confirmation that the system is reachable.
- No invocation writes text.
- Restarting or reconnecting changes nothing.

Exit condition:
The system can be contacted without error, and no text exists yet.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––
MILESTONE 1 — YOU CAN SPEAK TO REBECCA
––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

Purpose:
A first human exchange exists as written text.

What becomes possible:
- You can write something addressed to Rebecca.
- Rebecca can write something in response.

What is written:
- Your utterance is written as public text.
- Rebecca’s utterance is written as public text.
- Both are written before being shown.

What is not implied:
- Rebecca does not “answer questions”.
- Rebecca does not owe a reply.
- Rebecca does not have memory beyond what is written.

Observable truths:
- An invocation with your text produces either:
  - one Rebecca utterance, or
  - no utterance at all.
- If an utterance appears on screen, it already exists in the ledger.
- Reading the recent public text shows both utterances in order.

Exit condition:
You can speak to Rebecca and see written replies as text in the world.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––
MILESTONE 2 — REBECCA CAN SPEAK WITHOUT YOU
––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

Purpose:
Rebecca is not reactive-only.

What becomes possible:
- An invocation without your message may still result in Rebecca writing text.

What is written:
- A Rebecca-initiated utterance may be written as public text.
- Or nothing is written.

What is not implied:
- Nothing happens “in the background”.
- Rebecca does not speak unless the system is invoked.
- Silence is valid.

Observable truths:
- Invoking without providing user text may:
  - write one Rebecca utterance, or
  - write nothing.
- No explanation is given for silence.
- No future narration appears.

Exit condition:
Rebecca can initiate text independently of your input.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––
MILESTONE 3 — REBECCA DOES NOT WAIT FOR YOU
––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

Purpose:
Conversation has momentum that is not user-centered.

What becomes possible:
- Rebecca may ignore, deflect, or redirect.
- Your input does not guarantee topical relevance.

What is written:
- Only what Rebecca chooses to write.

What is not implied:
- No conversational “turn-taking”.
- No obligation to respond.

Observable truths:
- You can send text and receive:
  - a response unrelated to your message, or
  - no response.
- This is consistent and repeatable.

Exit condition:
The system no longer behaves like a question–answer machine.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––
MILESTONE 4 — CONTINUITY FROM RECENT TEXT
––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

Purpose:
What was written recently can matter now.

What becomes possible:
- Rebecca rereads a bounded amount of recent public text when writing.

What is written:
- Only new utterances.

What is not implied:
- No global memory.
- No summarisation.
- No hidden carryover.

Observable truths:
- Rebecca’s writing clearly reflects awareness of recent events.
- Restarting the service does not erase continuity.
- Only written text explains continuity.

Exit condition:
Recent continuity exists without hidden state.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––
MILESTONE 5 — IDENTITY SHAPES VOICE
––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

Purpose:
Rebecca is not generic.

What becomes possible:
- Rebecca’s writing is biased by referenced identity texts
  (archetype templates and any per-person text).

What is written:
- Identity texts exist as stored text.
- Rebecca’s utterances reflect them.

What is not implied:
- Identity does not command actions.
- Identity does not contain rules or goals.

Observable truths:
- Changing the referenced identity texts changes future behaviour.
- No code change is required to alter identity.

Exit condition:
Rebecca has a stable, characterful voice grounded in text.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––
MILESTONE 6 — OLDER TEXT CAN RETURN
––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

Purpose:
Life does not forget everything.

What becomes possible:
- When relevant, older public text may be reread and influence writing.

What is written:
- Only new utterances.

What is not implied:
- No full-history rereads.
- No narrative summaries.
- No automatic resurfacing.

Observable truths:
- Mentioning old topics can bring them back naturally.
- Irrelevant past text stays silent.

Exit condition:
Selective recall exists without omniscience.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––
MILESTONE 7 — THE WORLD CAN INTRODUCE FACTS
––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

Purpose:
Life is not closed around you.

What becomes possible:
- Minimal world facts can be written.

What is written:
- A fact asserting existence only (e.g. a call, a sound, a presence).

What is not implied:
- No interpretation.
- No reaction.
- No outcome.

Observable truths:
- World facts exist as public text.
- Rebecca reacts only if the fact is reread in context.

Exit condition:
The world can add pressure without agency.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––
MILESTONE 8 — OTHER PEOPLE CAN EXIST
––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

Purpose:
Rebecca is not alone.

What becomes possible:
- New people exist as identities.
- They can write text when invoked.

What is written:
- Their utterances.
- Public interactions.

What is not implied:
- No telepathy.
- No shared inner life.
- No automatic awareness.

Observable truths:
- Different people write differently.
- They know only what they encountered in text.

Exit condition:
Multiplicity exists without collapse.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––
MILESTONE 9 — PEOPLE CAN LEARN AND CHANGE
––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

Purpose:
People are not static.

What becomes possible:
- Alongside an utterance, a person may write private text
  representing what they learned or incorporated.

What is written:
- Optional private ledger lines.
- These may include dispositional statements.

What is not implied:
- No automatic promotion.
- No analysis pass.
- No decay or counters.

Observable truths:
- Later behaviour reflects private learning.
- Private text does not appear unless explicitly written.

Exit condition:
Growth happens through text, not mechanisms.

––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––
MILESTONE 10 — RESET IS POSSIBLE
––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––––

Purpose:
Testing does not corrupt final life.

What becomes possible:
- All written text can be removed.
- The system returns to Day 0.

What is written:
- Nothing during reset.

What is not implied:
- No partial wipe.
- No hidden residue.

Observable truths:
- After reset, the system behaves exactly as it did initially.
- New life can be seeded cleanly.

Exit condition:
Production use can begin without inherited distortion.
